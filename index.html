Parametric Seashell

Nature as engineer and artist. I strove to dive deep into just one of the countless ways we find mathematics in nature.

This was also an opportunity to dive into 3D on the web, which took me back to my roots as a mechanical engineer who loves to design in CAD. I wrote the first iteration in three.js, but had to pare back features because of the heavy computational needs. Fortunately, an expert guided me to regl and I ended up learning glsl and WebGL to create something much more in-line with my original vision.

How Bob Ross's Paintings Changed (and Didn't) Over Time

After finding this dataset I got inspired to make a chart that would mimic one of Bob Ross's beautiful landscape paintings. Like in a forest, the audience has to explore layer upon layer to find insights, which I evoked with overlapping transparent area graphs that fade in and out as the reader sifts through.

I found that in the first 17 seasons of Ross's show he didn't seem to have a consistent pattern of elements. In the last 15 seasons, however, he settled into a steady rhythm. Season 17 is also the most different season, perhaps reflecting a turning point in his life or approach to his craft.

Which Countries Have the Highest Incarceration Rate?

After seeing this data I realized that a simple and engaging visualization could show how extreme the incarceration system in the US is. The original data had many categories of metrics, but this one seemed to be the most telling. Sometimes less data is more.

I learned d3-force to create a dynamic beeswarm plot, with a color scheme going from sunny-yellow to inflamed-red. It highlights how extreme incarceration has become, with the US being in the company of authoritarian and oppressive regimes.

Population With Internet Access in 2017

In this chart I learned how to combine country data with geographical data. Internet access has its pros and cons, so I thought it would be interesting to see how it varies around the world. Maps are also right at the intersection of the natural world and human society, so I'm excited to make more use of them.

CEO vs. Worker Pay Over Time

This is part visualization/part slide deck. My goal was to create a storytelling experience that would guide an audience through the data surrounding CEO and worker pay over the years. I wanted to explore ways to draw a reader through. In the end, I think this went too far in thinking for the audience rather than showing the audience, but I learned a lot in the process and have used what I learned in other ways, like with the seashell explorable up top.
